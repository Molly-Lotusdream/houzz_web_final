  • HZer Profile: Em Gunther,
  • Customer Support
  • Connections play a big role in Em Gunther’s life. Em, who uses the pronoun they, spends their days
  • connecting with HZ customers as a customer support advocate. It was a chance meeting with
  • two HZ employees that showed Em why HZ was the place for them. And, as a co-lead of the
  • LGBTQ+ Life@HZ employee resource group, Em creates opportunities to bring together
  • HZers from around the world. When they’re not finding the best solutions for our customers, Em
  • can be found eating a good meal with friends, drinking an excellent cup of coffee and connecting
  • puzzle pieces with their girlfriend.
    • How did you find out about HZ?
  • I worked in the office building in Phoenix where HZ was located, and one day I ran into two
  • HZ employees who were LGBTQIA, like myself. We struck up a conversation, and I was in awe
  • when they talked about how they could be themselves at work. It blew my mind. These individuals
  • felt so loved at HZ. I had a feeling the company would welcome and accept you no matter what
  • – whoever you are, however you are. A couple of years later, I was looking at job boards and
  • applied to a posted customer support position. It came full circle.
    • What about the role was interesting to you?
  • I come from a family of service workers – and we like to talk to people! I love interacting with
  • customers and creating great relationships. This job requires me to use my communication skills,
  • and work both independently and with my team. The true perk of my job is providing solutions for
  • our customers. It was also exciting to know I was joining a company that would allow me to be me.
    • What do you enjoy most about HZ?
  • It’s the culture; it really is a company that cares. Even as it’s grown and expanded, the core values –
  • especially “We’re a family” – have remained the same. I listened to [cofounder and CEO] Adi speak
  • about how her phone is turned off at 6:30 during dinner with her family, and it showed that the
  • company values are held personally by the CEO. Our leadership team practices what it preaches,
  • and that’s so important to me.
    • What’s it like working here?
  • I’m very proud to work for HZ. It’s a badge of honor for me. HZ has proven that it’s loving,
  • accepting and kind, which is just what I was looking for in a company and for my career. I’m
  • engaged and energized coming to work every day. HZ appreciates you and what you do, and
  • the company shows that you’re valued through things like professional development courses and
  • employee resource groups (ERGs). You can tell HZ wants to make a positive impact on people
  • while they are here.
    • Speaking of ERGs, what’s the LGBTQ+ Life@HZ group?
  • We’re creating a space where LGBTQIA+ employees feel comfortable sharing their stories and
  • connecting with others. We want to support HZ’s efforts to recruit and retain LGBTQIA+-identified
  • employees and to maintain a positive, loving community that educates, reflects and acts on
  • LGBTQIA+ history and rights. We’re also focusing on networking, educational and social
  • opportunities, like encouraging our members to use our annual Volunteer Day off to support
  • LGBTQIA+ charities in the name of our group. My goal is to recreate for others the feeling I had
  • when I first discovered HZ – helping them know this company sees you for you, and you are
  • welcomed.
    • Why did you choose to co-lead the group?
  • I’ve been searching for a sense of community for a long time, and it seemed like a great opportunity.
  • I was lucky enough that two others also wanted to help lead, because I really wanted to be part of a
  • team.
    • Why is it important for companies to have employee resource groups?
  • It’s one thing to tell employees you care, and it’s another thing to show them. The ERG groups
  • solidify your place in the company. You get to meet people who are like-minded from all over the
  • world. With the Healthy Relationships and the LGBTQ+ groups, we really need to be able to find
  • each other to connect and talk. It’s even more important in this virtual world.
    • What do you do when you’re not working?
  • I’m going to Mesa Community College to get my associate’s degree in Applied Science for General
  • Business. I’d love to be a small-business owner, and have a farm and campground/bed and
  • breakfast someday. I like being cozy and in comfortable spaces, and I want to do that for others, too.
    • Speaking of cozy spaces, have you used HZ yourself?
  • Yes! We bought our entertainment stand from the HZ shop. It’s absolutely gorgeous. It was easy
  • to order, arrived perfectly and was straightforward to assemble. I love it!
